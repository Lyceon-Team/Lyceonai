# Comprehensive SAT Knowledge Base

## SAT Test Overview and Structure

### Digital SAT Format
The SAT is a digital standardized test administered through the Bluebook app. The test consists of 2 main sections with 4 modules total, lasting 2 hours and 14 minutes.

**Test Structure:**
- **Section 1: Reading and Writing** - 64 minutes total
  - Module 1: 32 minutes, 27 questions
  - Module 2: 32 minutes, 27 questions
- **Section 2: Math** - 70 minutes total  
  - Module 1: 35 minutes, 22 questions
  - Module 2: 35 minutes, 22 questions
- **Break**: 10-minute break between sections
- **Total**: 98 questions, 2 hours 14 minutes

### Adaptive Testing System
The digital SAT uses an adaptive testing model where student performance on Module 1 determines the difficulty level of Module 2.

**Adaptive Logic:**
- Strong performance on Module 1 → Higher difficulty Module 2
- Moderate performance on Module 1 → Medium difficulty Module 2  
- Weaker performance on Module 1 → Lower difficulty Module 2
- Each student receives questions appropriate to their demonstrated ability level
- Scoring algorithm accounts for question difficulty to ensure fair comparison

### Technology Requirements

**Bluebook Application:**
- Official SAT testing application developed by College Board
- Required for all digital SAT administrations
- Must be downloaded and configured before test day
- Includes built-in calculator, reference sheet, and annotation tools
- Operates in secure, locked-down mode during testing

**Device Requirements:**
- School-managed devices (preferred) or personal devices
- Windows, Mac, iPad, or Chromebook compatibility
- Stable internet connection required for download and setup
- Offline functionality during actual test administration
- Battery backup recommended (power outlets may not be available)

## SAT Content Areas and Skills

### Reading and Writing Section (800 points maximum)

**Question Types and Distribution:**
1. **Craft and Structure** (13-15 questions)
   - Words in context vocabulary
   - Text structure and purpose analysis
   - Cross-text connections and comparisons

2. **Information and Ideas** (12-14 questions)  
   - Central ideas and details
   - Command of textual evidence
   - Inferences and interpretations

3. **Standard English Conventions** (11-15 questions)
   - Sentence boundaries and structure
   - Subject-verb agreement
   - Verb forms and tenses
   - Pronouns and pronoun-antecedent agreement
   - Punctuation usage

4. **Expression of Ideas** (8-12 questions)
   - Rhetorical synthesis and transitions
   - Logical sequence and cohesion
   - Style, tone, and syntax

### Math Section (800 points maximum)

**Mathematical Domains:**
1. **Algebra** (35% of math questions)
   - Linear equations in one variable
   - Linear equations in two variables  
   - Linear functions and systems
   - Linear inequalities

2. **Advanced Math** (35% of math questions)
   - Equivalent expressions and equations
   - Nonlinear equations in one variable
   - Systems of equations in two variables
   - Nonlinear functions

3. **Problem-Solving and Data Analysis** (15% of math questions)
   - Ratios, rates, proportional relationships, and units
   - Percentages and percent change
   - One-variable data distributions and measures
   - Two-variable data relationships
   - Probability and conditional probability
   - Inference from sample statistics and margin of error

4. **Geometry and Trigonometry** (15% of math questions)
   - Area and volume calculations
   - Lines, angles, and triangles
   - Right triangles and basic trigonometry
   - Circles and geometric relationships

### Mathematical Tools Available
- Built-in graphing calculator (Desmos)
- Available for entire Math section
- Reference sheet with formulas
- Scratch paper allowed at test centers

## SAT Scoring System

### Score Scale and Ranges
- **Total Score Range**: 400-1600 points
- **Section Scores**: 200-800 points each (Reading & Writing + Math)
- **Subscores**: Provided for specific skill areas
- **Cross-test Scores**: Analysis in History/Social Studies and Science

### Score Reporting Timeline
- Scores typically available 2-3 weeks after test date
- Multiple-choice scores available first
- Score reports include percentiles and benchmarks
- Score choice policy allows students to send best scores

### College Readiness Benchmarks
- **Evidence-Based Reading and Writing**: 480
- **Math**: 530  
- **Total Benchmark**: 1010
- Students meeting benchmarks have 75% chance of earning C+ or higher in first-year college courses

### Percentile Rankings
Scores are reported with national and state percentiles showing how students performed relative to their peers.

## Testing Rules and Policies

### Legal Framework and Consequences
Taking the SAT constitutes entering into a legal agreement with College Board. Test security violations can result in:
- Score cancellation without refund
- Prohibition from future test registrations  
- Notification to colleges and scholarship programs
- Legal action for serious violations
- Criminal prosecution for fraud or impersonation

### Prohibited Items and Behaviors
**Strictly Forbidden:**
- Communication with other test takers during testing
- Sharing questions or answers in any format
- Using unauthorized devices or aids
- Taking photos or recording test content
- Attempting to access restricted areas of testing software
- Providing false identification or impersonation
- Bringing prohibited items into test room

**Prohibited Items:**
- Phones (unless used as testing device with restrictions)
- Smartwatches and fitness trackers
- External calculators (built-in calculator provided)
- Reference materials or notes
- Food and drinks (except during break)
- Bags, backpacks, or purses in testing area

### Academic Integrity Requirements
- Students must verify identity with valid photo ID
- Test content is confidential and copyrighted
- Sharing or reproducing test questions violates copyright law
- All work must be original and independent
- Collaboration or assistance from others is prohibited

## Test Day Procedures

### Registration and Check-in
- Arrive at test center by reporting time (typically 7:45 AM)
- Present valid photo identification
- Verify registration information
- Surrender prohibited items
- Receive seat assignment

### Testing Environment
- Assigned seating with adequate space
- Proctored by trained test administrators
- Security cameras may be present
- Disruptions minimized through strict protocols
- Accommodations provided when pre-approved

### Break Procedures
- 10-minute break between Reading & Writing and Math sections
- Students must remain in designated areas
- Access to snacks and drinks permitted during break
- No access to phones or prohibited items
- Restroom breaks allowed with proctor escort

### Technical Support
- On-site technical coordinator available
- Backup devices ready for hardware issues
- Test resumption procedures for technical problems
- Extended time for technical interruptions
- Alternative testing arrangements when needed

## Score Use and College Admissions

### How Colleges Use SAT Scores
- Admissions decision factor along with GPA, activities, essays
- Merit-based scholarship consideration
- Course placement in college mathematics and English
- Comparison tool among applicants from different schools
- Validation of academic preparation for college-level work

### Test-Optional Policies
Many colleges have adopted test-optional policies where:
- SAT scores are not required for admission
- Students can choose whether to submit scores
- Other factors may carry more weight in admissions
- Some programs still require scores (honors, merit scholarships)

### SuperScore Policies
Some colleges use "superscoring" which means:
- Taking the highest section scores across multiple test dates
- Creating a new composite score from best performances
- Allowing students to demonstrate improvement over time
- May require sending all scores or allow score choice

- Phased implementation beginning March 2023 internationally
- Complete transition in US by March 2024
- Shorter test duration (2 hours 14 minutes vs. 3 hours)
- Adaptive testing replaces single fixed-difficulty version
- Enhanced security through digital delivery

### Content Updates
- Reduced emphasis on obscure vocabulary
- More relevant reading passages and contexts
- Calculator allowed throughout Math section
- Streamlined question formats and types
- Updated scoring scale maintains comparability
